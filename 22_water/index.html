The video below shows that we're most of the way there! <video controls> <source src="./pH_demo.mp4" type="video/mp4"> </video> The stainless steel bowl represents the water tank to be tested, and the clear plastic container is a waste receptacle. A small-diameter pump In the demo, the water enters the testing chamber and then is removed. This could still leave some residue in the testing chamber. The video speed is 10x -- so in reality it takes closer to 2 minutes to perform a test. We can speed that up quite a bit if necessary (for example, use a larger diameter pump for the testing solution, user a higher voltage for the water pump, etc.). But maybe it's okay if it takes that much time? Next steps: * Modify peristaltic pump design to be a bit looser (currently requires too much force, occasionally skips steps). Design reservoir to hold pH testing solution. * Design PCB to hold all electronics. How many pumps should it be able to power, eventually? * Work on programming, image processing
